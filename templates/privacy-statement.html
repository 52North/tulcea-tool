Data privacy statement

The protection of personal data is extremely important for the Danube Delta National Institute for Research and Development (hereinafter referred to as INCDDD Tulcea). We are aware of our responsibility to protect your personal data and treat them under current data protection regulations laws. Our privacy statement provides an overview of how we collect and process personal data, use your personal data and how we intentionally transmit data to third parties.

We collect personal data when you use the Creating Interfaces - Tulcea website. Personal data is data that can be used to identify your person or that can be assigned to you (for example, name, e-mail address, IP address, telephone number). The purpose of the collection is purely scientific and excludes in any way the commercial side.

We have adopted technical and organizational measures to ensure that we and our external service providers comply with data confidentiality rules. There may be security breaches during the transfer of data over the Internet. Full protection against third party access is not possible.

We treat your data confidentially. Our data privacy statement provides an overview of the data we collect, how and why we collect it.

The rights of the person concerned

You have the right to request information about what kind of personal data we have stored and for what purpose they are used. You also have the right to correct or delete your personal data or to restrict its processing, as well as the right to oppose its processing. The right of opposition may also apply to certain processing of your personal data, such as direct e-mail marketing.

You may object to the use of your personal data at any time by letter or e-mail to iulian.nichersu@ddni.ro.

You also have the right to receive the relevant personal data that you have provided to us in a structured, standard and automatically readable format. At your request, we will forward your request to other responsible courts, as far as this is technically feasible.

If you do not feel sufficiently supported by our company, you have the right to appeal to a supervisory authority.

Transmission of personal data to a third country

The transmission of data to third countries (countries outside the European Union and the EEA) is done in the framework of the performance of the contract, the execution of pre-contractual measures and the necessary communication, taking into account the Adequacy Resolution of the EU Commission.

Data storage time

To the extent necessary, we process and store your personal data during the Belmont-Creating Interfaces Project. The data will be deleted after the expiration of the legal, legal or contractual retention periods. We will delete data that is not subject to the retention requirement after the purpose limitation has ceased.

Your personal data is collected either voluntarily (for example, through comments or contact forms), or automatically when you use the Creating Interfaces - Tulcea website. We collect these data for scientific purposes only, in order to highlight the existing or possible connections in the city of Tulcea between the three elements studied Food-Water-Energy.

Cookies

Cookies are used to optimize a website and to improve your user experience. Cookies are small text files that your browser stores when you visit web pages and applications. Your browser downloads cookies the first time you visit a specific website. When you review this website with the same device, the data stored in the cookie is sent back to the website that created it. This, in particular, serves to make our web offerings easier to use, more efficient and more secure.

You can specify how websites use cookies through the privacy settings in your browser (for more information on configuring cookie settings, see your browser's help function). You can also enable automatic deletion of cookies when you close your browser. However, this may lead to limited use of our web offerings.

You can find detailed information about cookies on the Internet.

Externally loaded functions

When you upload the Creating Interfaces - Tulcea site, your browser may be loading fonts and features (JavaScript) from external CDN servers (content delivery networks). Your browser saves them in the cache so that they do not reappear unless your browser cache is cleared.
